# Feeder
The feeder meters product into the packaging line. Important signals include feed rate, queue level, motor current, and starvation indicators. Likely failure modes include starvation, blockage, and sensor drift. Feeder problems can starve every downstream station.

# Filler
The filler dispenses product into containers. Important signals include temperature, vibration, power, throughput, and fill timing. Likely failure modes include overheating, high vibration, blockage, and mechanical wear. Filler problems reduce throughput and can create queues upstream.

# Sealer
The sealer closes or seals containers before labeling. Important signals include temperature, dwell timing, vibration, and throughput. Likely failure modes include overheating, jam risk, and mechanical wear. Sealer instability can create downstream reject rate and upstream accumulation.

# Labeler
The labeler applies or verifies labels. Important signals include alignment sensor state, model uncertainty, throughput, and reject rate. Likely failure modes include sensor drift, label feed issues, and mild throughput drop. Labeler problems may increase rejects and reduce confidence.

# Checker
The checker inspects product quality. Important signals include reject rate, vision status, queue level, and throughput. Likely failure modes include sensor drift, false rejects, and blockage. Checker problems can stop product flow or create unnecessary rejects.

# Packer
The packer or case packer groups items into cases. Important signals include case queue, throughput, vibration, and cycle timing. Likely failure modes include queue congestion, jam risk, and mechanical wear. Case packer bottlenecks often reduce full line throughput.

# Palletizer
The palletizer stacks cases for outbound handling. Important signals include robot current, queue level, grip status, and cycle timing. Likely failure modes include starvation, queue congestion, and mechanical wear. Palletizer slowdown can back up case packing.

# Wrapper
The wrapper secures pallet loads. Important signals include film tension, cycle time, motor current, and queue level. Likely failure modes include jam risk, film break, and mechanical wear. Wrapper problems can block final outbound flow.
