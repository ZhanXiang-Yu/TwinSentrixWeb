OUR SOLUTION

Built Different
From the Ground Up

TwinSentrix is an intelligent cyber-physical decision-support system for factories and process plants. Instead of starting with expensive new sensors, we start with simulation: a plant-specific digital twin built from existing diagrams, equipment specs, process logic, and known failure modes.

This simulation-first approach helps manufacturers understand bottlenecks, downtime risk, equipment degradation, and future production states before committing to costly hardware deployments.

EXPLORE OUR WORK

Starting with
Packaging Lines

Packaging and bottling lines are the final — and most failure-prone — stage of nearly every manufacturing process. A single breakdown cascades backwards and halts the entire upstream plant. We are starting here because the problem is massive, the failure modes are simulatable, and our simulation-first approach eliminates the need for years of historical sensor data.

Failure Modes We Target
  • Conveyor jams — responsible for 78% of all conveyor stoppages; micro-stops under 5 min consume up to 25% of available shift time
  • Filler head failures — nozzle clogging and seal wear; food & beverage fillers achieve only 29% median uptime, creating a 2.4× synchronization gap with downstream packagers
  • Capper misalignment & chuck wear — under-torqued caps cause product leaks; over-torqued caps crack; worn crown capper bells cause carbonation loss
  • Sealer jaw drift — temperature drift produces weak seals; uncalibrated sensors silently reduce throughput by 10–15%
How TwinSentrix Solves It
  • Digital twin of the packaging line — conveyor, filler, capper, sealer — built from existing machine specs, no new hardware required
  • Synthetic failure data generated by injecting bearing wear, jam probability, and cycle time drift into the physics-accurate simulation
  • LSTM model trained on simulation data predicts failures 2+ weeks ahead — no years of historical sensor data needed to start
  • AI Agent alerts operators in plain language: "Conveyor C-02 bearing wear detected — failure likely within 8 days"
